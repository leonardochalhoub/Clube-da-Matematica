<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Asymptotes — definitions and computation">

### Vertical asymptote (VA)

<Definicao titulo="Vertical asymptote">
The line $x = a$ is a VA of $f$ if $\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = \pm\infty$ or $\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = \pm\infty$.
</Definicao>

**Where they appear**: points where $f$ has a pole (zero denominator, log of 0, etc.).

### Horizontal asymptote (HA)

<Definicao titulo="Horizontal asymptote">
The line $y = L$ is an HA of $f$ if $\lim_{x \to +\infty} f(x) = L$ or $\lim_{x \to -\infty} f(x) = L$.
</Definicao>

**There may be two HAs** (one at $+\infty$, another at $-\infty$): for example $\arctan x$ has $y = \pi/2$ and $y = -\pi/2$.

### Oblique asymptote (OA)

<Definicao titulo="Oblique asymptote">
The line $y = mx + b$ ($m \neq 0$) is an OA if $\lim_{x \to \pm\infty} (f(x) - (mx + b)) = 0$.
</Definicao>

**Computation**:
$$m = \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} \frac{f(x)}{x}, \quad b = \lim_{x \to \pm\infty} (f(x) - mx).$$

If both limits exist with finite non-zero $m$, there is an OA.

### Asymptote table for $P(x)/Q(x)$

| Case | Asymptotes |
|---|---|
| $\deg P < \deg Q$ | HA at $y = 0$ |
| $\deg P = \deg Q$ | HA at $y = $ ratio of leading coeffs |
| $\deg P = \deg Q + 1$ | OA ($y = $ quotient of division) |
| $\deg P > \deg Q + 1$ | No OA (grows faster) |

### Other functions

| Function | Asymptote |
|---|---|
| $1/x$ | VA $x = 0$, HA $y = 0$ |
| $e^x$ | HA $y = 0$ ($x \to -\infty$) |
| $\ln x$ | VA $x = 0$ |
| $\arctan x$ | HA $y = \pm\pi/2$ |
| $\tan x$ | VA at $\pi/2 + k\pi$ |
| $1/\sin x$ | VA at $k\pi$ |

### General procedure

1. **VA**: find points where $f$ is undefined (denom. = 0). Compute one-sided limit.
2. **HA**: compute $\lim_{x \to \pm\infty} f$.
3. **OA**: if no HA, compute $m, b$ via the formulas.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="5" titulo="For a 5-year-old">

Imagine a road approaching a **wall** but never touching. The wall is the **asymptote** — the function gets close but doesn't touch.

There are three types of wall:
- **Vertical** (standing): the function rises or falls a lot near it.
- **Horizontal** (lying down): the function flattens and becomes nearly straight at the end.
- **Oblique** (slanted): the function turns into a slanted line far away.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="10" titulo="For a 10-year-old">

Think of a **stretched graph**: far away it looks like something simple (a horizontal, vertical, or slanted line). That "simplification" is the **asymptote**.

**Examples**:

- $f(x) = 1/x$:
  - **VA**: $x = 0$ — near zero, the function blows up.
  - **HA**: $y = 0$ — for large $x$, $1/x \to 0$.

- $f(x) = (x+1)/(x-1)$:
  - **VA**: $x = 1$.
  - **HA**: $y = 1$ (for $x \to \infty$, denom. and num. both $\to \infty$, ratio $\to 1$).

- $f(x) = (x^2 + 1)/x$:
  - **VA**: $x = 0$.
  - **OA**: $y = x$ (because $(x^2+1)/x = x + 1/x$, and $1/x \to 0$).

**Use to sketch graphs**: identify asymptotes, mark them, and draw the curve approaching them.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old teen">

Asymptotic analysis is the **standard tool** for sketching graphs. In four steps:

### Sketching procedure

1. **Domain**: where is $f$ defined?
2. **Vertical asymptotes**: points outside the domain. Compute $\lim_{x \to a^\pm} f$.
3. **Horizontal/oblique asymptotes**: behavior at $\pm\infty$.
4. **Special points**: zeros, local max/min.

### Complete example: $f(x) = (x^2 + 1)/(x - 2)$

**1. Domain**: $\mathbb{R} \setminus \{2\}$.

**2. VA**: at $x = 2$. $\lim_{x \to 2^+} = +\infty$, $\lim_{x \to 2^-} = -\infty$. VA: $x = 2$.

**3. OA**: polynomial division: $(x^2+1)/(x-2) = x + 2 + 5/(x-2)$. As $x \to \pm\infty$, $5/(x-2) \to 0$. OA: $y = x + 2$.

**4. Sketch**: graph approaches $y = x + 2$ far away, "explodes" near $x = 2$.

### Special cases

- **Even/odd function**: asymptotes mirror the symmetry. $f(-x) = f(x)$ → asymptotes at $\pm a$.
- **Periodic**: $\tan x$ has VA at every $\pi/2 + k\pi$.
- **Function that oscillates at infinity**: $\sin x$ has no HA (oscillates between $-1$ and $1$, doesn't tend to a single value).

### Mnemonic table for rationals

$f(x) = P(x)/Q(x)$, $\deg P = m$, $\deg Q = n$:

| Case | HA | OA |
|---|---|---|
| $m < n$ | $y = 0$ | No |
| $m = n$ | $y = a_m/b_n$ | No |
| $m = n+1$ | No | $y = $ quotient of division |
| $m > n+1$ | No | No (but there is polynomial asymptotic behavior) |

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="For an engineering student">

Asymptotic analysis is central in **Bode diagrams**, **high-frequency response**, **stability**.

### Rigorous OA calculation

$f(x) = mx + b + g(x)$ where $g(x) \to 0$. Then $m = \lim f/x$ and $b = \lim (f - mx)$.

**Proof**:
- $f/x = m + b/x + g(x)/x \to m$.
- $f - mx = b + g(x) \to b$.

Each limit **separately** must exist and be finite (with $m$ allowed to be zero, in which case it becomes HA).

### VA via singularities

For rational $f = P/Q$: VA at zeros of $Q$ that are **not** zeros of $P$ (otherwise, removable).

For $f = \tan x = \sin x / \cos x$: VA at zeros of $\cos x$ (i.e., $\pi/2 + k\pi$).

### Application in dynamical systems

- **Transfer function**: $H(s) = (s+1)/((s+2)(s+3))$. At $s = -2, -3$, poles (VA in the complex plane). Behavior $\to 0$ for $|s| \to \infty$.
- **Bode plot**: $|H(j\omega)|$ vs $\omega$ in log-log. Slopes at high/low frequency are **oblique asymptotes** in log scale.

**Bode example**: $H(s) = K/(s+1)$. As $\omega \to 0$: $|H| \to K$ (low-freq HA). As $\omega \to \infty$: $|H| \approx K/\omega$, i.e., $\log|H| \approx \log K - \log \omega$ (OA of slope $-20$ dB/dec).

### Applications in control

- **Type 0**: $\lim_{s \to 0} H(s) = K$ (finite) — steady-state error to ramp = $\infty$.
- **Type 1**: $\lim_{s \to 0} sH(s) = K$ — error to step = 0, to ramp = $1/K$.
- **Type 2**: $\lim_{s \to 0} s^2 H(s) = K$ — error to ramp = 0.

### Applications in finance

Black-Scholes as $T \to 0$: option $\to \max(S - K, 0)$. **Asymptote** capturing the intrinsic payoff. As $T \to \infty$: complicated, depends on volatility.

### Algebraic curves

$x^2/a^2 + y^2/b^2 = 1$ (ellipse): no asymptotes.
$x^2/a^2 - y^2/b^2 = 1$ (hyperbola): asymptotes $y = \pm (b/a) x$.
$y^2 = x^3 - x$ (cubic): asymptotes depend on branch behavior.

### Multidimensional analysis

In $\mathbb{R}^n$, asymptotes become **asymptotic directions** or **asymptotic planes**. For $f(x, y) \to L$ as $\|(x, y)\| \to \infty$, $z = L$ is a **horizontal asymptotic plane**.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="40" titulo="For a senior professional">

Asymptotic analysis is an entire area in applied mathematics — goes far beyond elementary asymptotes.

### Big-O / little-o notation

$f(x) = O(g(x))$: $|f| \leq C|g|$ in a neighborhood.
$f(x) = o(g(x))$: $\lim f/g = 0$.
$f \sim g$: $\lim f/g = 1$.

Allows comparison of **growth scales** rigorously. **Stirling**: $n! \sim \sqrt{2\pi n} (n/e)^n$.

### Laplace asymptotic analysis

For $\int e^{Mf(x)} dx$ with $M \to \infty$, contribution comes from the maximum of $f$:
$$\int e^{Mf(x)} dx \sim \sqrt{\frac{2\pi}{M|f''(x_0)|}} e^{Mf(x_0)}.$$

Application: probability of rare events, statistical mechanics.

### Stationary phase method

For $\int e^{iMf(x)} g(x) dx$, contribution at points where $f'(x) = 0$ (stationary phase). Application: optics, QM.

### Asymptotic expansions (non-convergent)

Stirling series for $\Gamma$:
$$\ln \Gamma(z) \sim (z - 1/2) \ln z - z + \ln \sqrt{2\pi} + \sum_{k=1}^\infty \frac{B_{2k}}{2k(2k-1) z^{2k-1}}.$$

Diverges, but **truncated** gives an excellent approximation.

### Asymptotic analysis in PDE

- **Boundary layer** (Prandtl): Navier-Stokes solution as $\nu \to 0$ — singular limit.
- **WKB methods** in Schrödinger's equation: $\hbar \to 0$.
- **Homogenization**: PDE with finely oscillating coefficients.

### Green's functions and asymptotes

For elliptic PDE in an infinite domain, the Green's solution decays at a specific rate (e.g., $1/r^{n-2}$ in $\mathbb{R}^n$). Radial asymptotes are exactly that.

### Numerical

- **Padé approximants**: optimized rational approximation — captures asymptotes better than Taylor.
- **Resummation**: techniques (Borel, Padé, Chebyshev) for extracting information from divergent series.
- **Asymptotic acceleration**: Richardson, Aitken, Wynn.

### Applications in ML

- **Hyperparameter scaling**: scaling laws for LLMs (Kaplan, Hoffmann, Chinchilla) are asymptotic analyses in $N, D, C$.
- **SGD convergence**: $\|w_t - w^*\| \sim C/\sqrt t$ or $\sim e^{-\rho t}$ depending on regime.
- **Generalization gap**: $O(\sqrt{d/n})$ via concentration.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="pratica" titulo="Practical application">

**Recipe: finding the asymptotes of $f$.**

### Algorithm

1. **Domain**: identify points outside.
2. **VA**: for each $a$ outside the domain, compute $\lim_{x \to a^\pm} f$. If infinite, VA at $x = a$.
3. **HA**: $\lim_{x \to +\infty} f$ and $\lim_{x \to -\infty} f$. If finite, respective HAs.
4. **OA**: if no HA at $\pm\infty$, compute $m = \lim f/x$ and $b = \lim (f - mx)$.

### SymPy

```python
import sympy as sp
x = sp.symbols('x')
f = (x**2 + 1)/(x - 2)
# VAs
print(sp.limit(f, x, 2, '+'))     # +oo
print(sp.limit(f, x, 2, '-'))     # -oo
# OAs
m = sp.limit(f/x, x, sp.oo)        # 1
b = sp.limit(f - m*x, x, sp.oo)    # 2
print(f"OA: y = {m}x + {b}")       # y = x + 2
```

### Graph sketching (step by step)

For $f(x) = x^3/(x^2 - 1)$:

1. **Domain**: $\mathbb{R} \setminus \{-1, 1\}$.
2. **VAs**: $x = -1$ and $x = 1$.
3. **HA**: $\lim_{x \to \infty} f = \infty$, no HA.
4. **OA**: $f/x = x^2/(x^2-1) \to 1$, $f - x = x^3/(x^2-1) - x = x/(x^2-1) \to 0$. OA: $y = x$.
5. **Zeros of $f$**: $x = 0$.
6. **Symmetry**: $f(-x) = -f(x)$, odd.

Sketch accordingly.

### Cases where there is NO conventional asymptote

- **$f$ oscillates at infinity**: $\sin x$, $x \sin x$.
- **Grows faster than linear**: $x^2$ has no OA.
- **Branches**: $\sqrt{x^2 + 1}$ has **different** OA at $+\infty$ and $-\infty$ ($y = x$ and $y = -x$).

### Multiple cases

$f(x) = \arctan x$ has **two** HAs: $y = \pi/2$ ($x \to +\infty$) and $y = -\pi/2$ ($x \to -\infty$).
