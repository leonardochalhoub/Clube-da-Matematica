<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Rigorous definitions">

### One-sided limits

<Definicao titulo="Right-hand limit">
$\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = L$ if $\forall \eps > 0, \exists \delta > 0 : 0 < x - a < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - L| < \eps$.
</Definicao>

<Definicao titulo="Left-hand limit">
$\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = L$ if $\forall \eps > 0, \exists \delta > 0 : 0 < a - x < \delta \Rightarrow |f(x) - L| < \eps$.
</Definicao>

### Existence theorem via one-sided limits

$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L \iff \lim_{x \to a^+} f(x) = \lim_{x \to a^-} f(x) = L$.

### Infinite limits

$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = +\infty$: $\forall M > 0, \exists \delta > 0 : 0 < |x - a| < \delta \Rightarrow f(x) > M$.

Analogous for $-\infty$ and one-sided.

### Limits at infinity

$\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = L$: $\forall \eps > 0, \exists N : x > N \Rightarrow |f(x) - L| < \eps$.

$\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x) = +\infty$: $\forall M, \exists N : x > N \Rightarrow f(x) > M$.

### Quantifier table

| Type | Form |
|---|---|
| $\lim_{x \to a} = L$ | $\forall \eps, \exists \delta$ |
| $\lim_{x \to a^+} = L$ | $\forall \eps, \exists \delta$, $x > a$ |
| $\lim_{x \to a} = \infty$ | $\forall M, \exists \delta$ |
| $\lim_{x \to \infty} = L$ | $\forall \eps, \exists N$ |
| $\lim_{x \to \infty} = \infty$ | $\forall M, \exists N$ |

### "Pass-through" rules for $P/Q$ as $x \to \infty$

| $\deg P$ vs $\deg Q$ | $\lim$ |
|---|---|
| $\deg P > \deg Q$ | $\pm\infty$ |
| $\deg P = \deg Q$ | ratio of leading coeffs |
| $\deg P < \deg Q$ | $0$ |

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="5" titulo="For a 5-year-old">

Imagine a door. You can reach it **from outside** (the right) or **from inside the house** (the left).

**One-sided limit** is looking from which side you come.

If from each side you see **the same thing**, the "normal" limit exists. If you see **different things**, there's no limit — only the one-sided ones.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="10" titulo="For a 10-year-old">

Think of **going up a staircase** versus **coming down a staircase**. At some points, it depends on which side you come from.

For example, $f(x) = 1/x$. If you approach $0$ from the right ($x = 0.1, 0.01$, ...), $f(x)$ goes to $+\infty$ (climbs up). If you come from the left ($x = -0.1, -0.01$, ...), it goes to $-\infty$ (drops down).

Since they give different answers, **there is no limit** as $x \to 0$. But there are **one-sided limits**:

- $\lim_{x \to 0^+} 1/x = +\infty$
- $\lim_{x \to 0^-} 1/x = -\infty$

**Golden rule**: a normal limit exists **if and only if** both one-sided limits exist **and are equal**.

**Limit at infinity**: what does $f(x)$ do when $x$ goes very far? $1/x$, for example, goes to zero as $x$ goes to $+\infty$.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old teen">

When a function has **different behavior** on each side of a point, we need to look **one-sided**.

### Classic examples

| $f(x)$ | $\lim_{x \to 0^+}$ | $\lim_{x \to 0^-}$ | $\lim_{x \to 0}$ exists? |
|---|---|---|---|
| $1/x$ | $+\infty$ | $-\infty$ | No |
| $|x|/x$ | $1$ | $-1$ | No (jump) |
| $\sqrt x$ | $0$ | undef. | Only from the right |
| $x \cdot \mathrm{sgn}(x)$ | $0$ | $0$ | Yes ($= 0$) |

### Infinite limits: vertical asymptotes

When $\lim_{x \to a^\pm} f(x) = \pm \infty$, there's a **vertical asymptote** $x = a$ (Lesson 47).

Examples: $1/(x-2)$ has VA at $x = 2$. $\tan x$ at $x = \pi/2$.

### Limits at infinity: asymptotic behavior

$\lim_{x \to \infty} f(x)$ can be:
- A finite number $L$ → horizontal asymptote $y = L$.
- $\pm \infty$ → grows/decreases without limit.
- Doesn't exist (oscillates like $\sin x$).

### Polynomial-over-polynomial table

For $\lim_{x \to \infty} (a_n x^n + ...)/(b_m x^m + ...)$:

- $n < m$: $\to 0$.
- $n = m$: $\to a_n/b_m$.
- $n > m$: $\to \pm\infty$.

### Numerical example

$\lim_{x \to \infty} (3x^2 + 1)/(x^2 - 1) = 3$ (equal degrees, ratio 3/1).

$\lim_{x \to \infty} x/(x^2 + 1) = 0$ (denom larger).

$\lim_{x \to \infty} (x^3 + 1)/x = +\infty$ (numer larger).

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="For an engineering student">

One-sided and infinite limits are essential in **system analysis**, **frequency response**, **stability**.

### ε-δ proof: $\lim_{x \to 0^+} 1/x = +\infty$

Given $M > 0$, choose $\delta = 1/M$. For $0 < x < \delta = 1/M$, $1/x > M$. ∎

### Proof: "pass-through" rule

For $P(x)/Q(x)$ with $\deg P = \deg Q = n$:
$$\frac{a_n x^n + ...}{b_n x^n + ...} = \frac{a_n + a_{n-1}/x + ...}{b_n + b_{n-1}/x + ...} \xrightarrow{x \to \infty} \frac{a_n}{b_n}.$$

### Applications in systems

- **DC gain** of a linear system: $H(0) = \lim_{s \to 0} H(s)$. For $H = (s+1)/((s+2)(s+3))$, $H(0) = 1/6$.
- **High-frequency gain**: $\lim_{|s| \to \infty} |H(s)|$. For low-pass, goes to zero. For high-pass, finite number.
- **Step response**: $y(\infty) = \lim_{t \to \infty} y(t) = $ final value (final value theorem).
- **Poles on the imaginary axis**: $H(s)$ with pole $j\omega_0$ → $|H(s)| \to \infty$ as $s \to j\omega_0$ (resonance).

### Asymptotic behavior in sequences

- $\lim_{n \to \infty} n^k/a^n = 0$ for $a > 1$ (exponential beats polynomial).
- $\lim_{n \to \infty} n!/n^n = 0$ (factorial does **not** beat $n^n$).
- $\lim_{n \to \infty} a^n/n! = 0$ (factorial beats exponential).

**Growth hierarchy**: $\log < \text{polynomial} < \text{exponential} < \text{factorial} < n^n$.

### Sign function and applications

$\mathrm{sgn}(x)$ in control: discontinuity at $x = 0$ → introduces **chattering** in sliding-mode control. Solution: replace with $\tanh(kx)$ (smooth).

### Infinite limits vs. asymptotes

Caution: $\lim_{x \to \infty} \sin x$ **does not exist** (oscillates), but $\lim_{x \to \infty} (\sin x)/x = 0$ (squeeze). Horizontal asymptote $y = 0$.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="40" titulo="For a senior professional">

One-sided and infinite limits have deep topological interpretations.

### Topology of an extended line

$\overline{\mathbb{R}} = \mathbb{R} \cup \{-\infty, +\infty\}$ with appropriate topology (neighborhoods of $+\infty$ are $(N, +\infty]$). Infinite limits become limits in $\overline{\mathbb{R}}$.

### Compactification

$\overline{\mathbb{R}}$ is compact (homeomorphic to $[-1, 1]$ via $x/\sqrt{1+x^2}$). Sequence limits always exist in $\overline{\mathbb{R}}$ via Bolzano-Weierstrass.

### Lim sup, lim inf

For sequences without limit: $\limsup a_n = \lim_{n \to \infty} \sup_{k \geq n} a_k$, $\liminf$ analogously. Always exist in $\overline{\mathbb{R}}$. $\lim$ exists ⇔ $\limsup = \liminf$.

### Directional limits in $\mathbb{R}^n$

In higher dimension, "one-sided" generalizes to **directional**: $\lim_{t \to 0^+} f(a + t v)$ for vector $v$. Existence of the multidimensional limit requires all directions to coincide.

Classic counterexample: $f(x, y) = xy/(x^2 + y^2)$ at $(0, 0)$. Directional limits exist (depend on direction), but **no global limit**.

### Residues and poles

$f(z)$ holomorphic with pole at $z_0$ has $\lim_{z \to z_0} f(z) = \infty$ on the Riemann sphere. Residue $\text{Res}_{z_0} f = \lim_{z \to z_0} (z - z_0) f(z)$ characterizes the simple pole.

### Applications in PDE

- **Boundary conditions**: PDE solution in a bounded domain must have well-defined limits at the boundary.
- **Fundamental solutions**: Green's function has infinite limit at the source (singular), finite far away.
- **Laplace asymptotics**: $\int e^{Mf(x)} dx \sim$ contribution of the maximum of $f$ as $M \to \infty$.

### Filters and nets in topology

One-sided limits are special cases of **filter** at $a$: right-side filter = filter of right-hand neighborhoods of $a$. Generalizes to general topologies.

### Numerical

- **Conditional vs absolute convergence**: $\sum a_n$ may converge but $\sum |a_n|$ may not — Riemann showed reordering changes the sum. Directional limits in series.
- **Analytic continuation**: complex limits define $\zeta(s)$, $\Gamma(s)$ for values where the series doesn't converge.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="pratica" titulo="Practical application">

**Recipe: one-sided and infinite limits.**

### When you need one-sided

- Function with a change at a point (piecewise defined).
- Function with a **hole** or **asymptote**.
- Verify the existence of the two-sided limit.

### Strategy for $\lim_{x \to a^\pm}$

1. Consider $x = a + \eps$ (small $\eps > 0$) for $a^+$.
2. Consider $x = a - \eps$ (small $\eps > 0$) for $a^-$.
3. Compute and see if they coincide.

### Strategy for $\lim_{x \to \infty}$

1. **Polynomial ratio**: divide everything by the highest denominator degree.
2. **Exponential vs polynomial**: exponential always wins.
3. **Logarithm**: grows slower than any polynomial.

### SymPy

```python
import sympy as sp
x = sp.symbols('x')
print(sp.limit(1/x, x, 0, '+'))            # +oo
print(sp.limit(1/x, x, 0, '-'))            # -oo
print(sp.limit((3*x + 1)/(x + 5), x, sp.oo))  # 3
print(sp.limit(x**2 / sp.exp(x), x, sp.oo))   # 0 (exp wins)
```

### Wolfram Alpha

`limit (x^2-1)/(x-1) as x -> 1 from below` → 2.

### When the limit DOES NOT exist

- One-sided exist but differ (jump).
- Some one-sided is $\pm\infty$ (but you can say $\lim = \pm\infty$ if both coincide).
- Oscillation: $\sin(1/x)$ at 0, $\sin x$ at $\infty$.

### Common care

- Forgetting to check one-sided → makes a mistake at discontinuities.
- Confusing "goes to infinity" with "limit doesn't exist" — in general, an **infinite** limit is a case of **non-existence** in the strict sense (the limit is not a number), but we write it by convention.
