<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Term roadmap">

This lesson does not introduce new content. It is an **integrative workshop** with 25 problems that require combining:

- **Lesson 1**: notation of sets and intervals
- **Lesson 2**: domain, range, composition
- **Lessons 3-4**: linear and quadratic functions
- **Lesson 5**: composition and inverse
- **Lessons 6-8**: exponential, logarithm, growth/decay models
- **Lesson 9**: average rate of change

Problem styles:
- **Combined application** (10): require 2-3 sequential concepts
- **Modeling** (8): translation of real-world statement into function + analysis
- **Challenge** (5): hard ENEM level, EJU, Abitur, Suneung
- **Proof** (2): consolidate proof-writing ability

### Suggested self-assessment

Set aside 4h with no references to solve all 25. Then check the answer key (25% have full worked solutions; the rest have numeric answers). If you score **less than 50%**, reread the corresponding lessons; if you score **70-90%**, you're ready for Term 2; **above 90%**, you can go beyond by reading the reference material in Readings.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="How to approach the problems">

**4-step solving strategy (applicable to all):**

1. **Identify** the type(s) of function involved.
2. **Determine** domain and range based on physical/real context.
3. **Solve** symbolically.
4. **Verify** with a concrete value: plug in an extreme value and see if it makes sense.

This recipe is what Japanese teachers call "**寧ろ**" (deliberate slowness): even if the problem is easy, going through the 4 steps avoids mistakes that automatic mode produces. In Bachillerato exams and university engineering, this routine is worth 30% of the grade.

</Porta>
