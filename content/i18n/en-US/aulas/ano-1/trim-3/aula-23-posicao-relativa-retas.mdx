<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Criteria and formulas">

### Parallelism and perpendicularity

Given lines $r: y = m_r x + n_r$ and $s: y = m_s x + n_s$:

- **Parallel (non-coincident)**: $m_r = m_s$ and $n_r \neq n_s$.
- **Coincident**: $m_r = m_s$ and $n_r = n_s$.
- **Intersecting**: $m_r \neq m_s$.
- **Perpendicular**: $m_r \cdot m_s = -1$ (assuming neither is vertical).

Cases with vertical lines: $x = a$ is vertical, $y = b$ is horizontal. Two verticals are parallel; vertical with horizontal are perpendicular.

### Angle between lines

$$\tan\theta = \left|\frac{m_r - m_s}{1 + m_r m_s}\right|$$

(Assuming neither is vertical.)

### Point-line distance

Point $P_0 = (x_0, y_0)$ and line $Ax + By + C = 0$:

$$d(P_0, r) = \frac{|A x_0 + B y_0 + C|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$$

### Distance between parallel lines

For $r: Ax + By + C_1 = 0$ and $s: Ax + By + C_2 = 0$:
$$d(r, s) = \frac{|C_1 - C_2|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2}}$$

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old teenager">

Two lines in the plane can:

1. **Be parallel** (same slope, never cross).
2. **Be perpendicular** (form a 90° angle).
3. **Cross at one point** (intersecting).
4. **Be the same line** (coincident).

**Parallelism criterion**: $m_r = m_s$ — same slope.

**Perpendicularity criterion**: $m_r \cdot m_s = -1$. Strange, but it comes from applying the Pythagorean theorem to the triangle formed by the two displacements.

**Example**: $y = 2x + 1$ and $y = -x/2 + 5$ are perpendicular ($2 \cdot (-1/2) = -1$).

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="For an engineering student">

In practice, **solving 2x2 linear systems** is the intersection of lines. Cramer and elimination start here.

### 2x2 system and geometric interpretation

$$\begin{cases} a_1 x + b_1 y = c_1 \\ a_2 x + b_2 y = c_2 \end{cases}$$

- **Unique solution**: intersecting lines. $\det \neq 0$.
- **Infinite solutions**: coincident lines. Determinant $= 0$ + consistent system.
- **No solution**: distinct parallel lines. Determinant $= 0$ + inconsistent system.

</Porta>
