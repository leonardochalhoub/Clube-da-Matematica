<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Vectors in ℝ²">

<Definicao titulo="Vector">
$\vec v \in \mathbb{R}^2$ is an ordered pair $(v_1, v_2)$. Geometrically, it represents **displacement** or **oriented direction**.
</Definicao>

### Operations

- **Sum**: $\vec u + \vec v = (u_1 + v_1, u_2 + v_2)$.
- **Scalar**: $\alpha \vec v = (\alpha v_1, \alpha v_2)$.
- **Subtraction**: $\vec u - \vec v = (u_1 - v_1, u_2 - v_2)$.

### Magnitude (norm)

$$|\vec v| = \sqrt{v_1^2 + v_2^2}$$

### Unit vector

$\hat v = \vec v / |\vec v|$ has magnitude 1. **Versor**.

### Canonical vectors

$\hat\imath = (1, 0)$, $\hat\jmath = (0, 1)$. Every vector: $\vec v = v_1 \hat\imath + v_2 \hat\jmath$.

### Properties

- **Commutative**: $\vec u + \vec v = \vec v + \vec u$.
- **Associative**: $(\vec u + \vec v) + \vec w = \vec u + (\vec v + \vec w)$.
- **Identity**: $\vec 0 = (0, 0)$, $\vec v + \vec 0 = \vec v$.
- **Opposite**: $\vec v + (-\vec v) = \vec 0$.
- **Distributive**: $\alpha(\vec u + \vec v) = \alpha \vec u + \alpha \vec v$.

These 8 properties characterize a **vector space** — seen formally in linear algebra (Term 12).

### Parallelogram rule

$\vec u + \vec v$ is the diagonal of the parallelogram formed by $\vec u$ and $\vec v$.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old teenager">

A vector is "an arrow" in the plane. It has:
- **Magnitude** (length of the arrow)
- **Direction** (the line on which it lies)
- **Orientation** (which end of the line)

In coordinates, it is just a pair $(a, b)$. The pair $(3, 4)$ is an arrow that goes 3 to the right and 4 up. Magnitude: $\sqrt{9 + 16} = 5$.

**Sum** is placing one arrow at the tip of the other. $(2,1) + (3,4) = (5, 5)$ — added component by component.

**Multiplication by scalar**: stretches or contracts. $3 \cdot (2, 1) = (6, 3)$ — three times bigger.

**Where it appears**: everything in physics that has direction is a vector. Velocity. Force. Acceleration. Electric field. Angular momentum. In games, character positions; in ML, word embedding is a vector of 100+ dimensions.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="For an engineering student">

The vector is the most important object of all applied mathematics. In $\mathbb{R}^n$:

- **Mechanics**: force is a vector, sum of forces is the resultant (parallelogram rule).
- **Kinematics**: velocity $\vec v$ and acceleration $\vec a$ are vectors; $\vec v(t) = \vec v_0 + \vec a t$ (vector composition).
- **Statics**: equilibrium = $\sum \vec F = \vec 0$ — system of vector equations.
- **Electromag**: electric/magnetic field; Lorentz force $\vec F = q\vec E + q\vec v \times \vec B$.

### Decomposition

Every vector in $\mathbb{R}^2$ decomposes **uniquely** into parallel and perpendicular components to a given direction — the basis of resolving forces on inclined planes.

</Porta>
