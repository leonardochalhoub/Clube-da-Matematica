<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Trim 2 Roadmap">

Workshop with 25 problems that require combining:

- **Lesson 11**: trigonometric ratios in a right triangle
- **Lesson 12**: trigonometric circle, radians
- **Lesson 13**: trigonometric functions and modeling
- **Lesson 14**: trigonometric equations and inequalities
- **Lesson 15**: laws of sines and cosines
- **Lessons 16-18**: sequences, AP, GP
- **Lesson 19**: intuitive limit

### Problem styles

- **Combined application** (10): trigonometry + function
- **Modeling** (8): translating real-world statements
- **Challenge** (5): hard ENEM or Olympiad level
- **Proof** (2): consolidating demonstrations

### Self-assessment

Set aside **4h with no consultation** to solve. Check the answer key (25% have a developed solution). If you score < 50%, reread the corresponding lessons; 70-90% ready for Trim 3; > 90%, additional reading from reference books.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="25" titulo="How to approach the problems">

**4-step strategy:**

1. **Identify** the problem type (triangle, periodic function, sequence, limit).
2. **Determine** the given information and what is asked.
3. **Choose** the tool (law of sines, AP formula, etc.).
4. **Verify** with a concrete value or sketch.

</Porta>

### Block A — Trigonometry (20.1 to 20.10)

<Exercicio numero="20.1" dificuldade="aplicacao">Solve $\sin(2x) = 1/2$ on $[0, 2\pi)$.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.2" dificuldade="aplicacao">Triangle with $a = 5$, $A = 30°$, $B = 60°$. Compute $b$ using the law of sines.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.3" dificuldade="aplicacao">Triangle with sides $7, 8, 9$. Largest angle?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.4" dificuldade="aplicacao">Sketch $y = 2\sin(\pi x/3)$ — period and amplitude.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.5" dificuldade="aplicacao">Compute $\sin(\pi/3)$, $\cos(7\pi/4)$, $\tan(5\pi/6)$.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.6" dificuldade="modelagem">Tide in Salvador: $h(t) = 1{,}5 + 1\sin(\pi t/6)$. When is $h = 1{,}5$? When is it maximum?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.7" dificuldade="modelagem">Grid voltage: $V(t) = 311 \sin(120\pi t)$ V. RMS voltage: $V_{ef} = V_0/\sqrt 2$. Compute approximate $V_{ef}$ (~220 V).</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.8" dificuldade="modelagem">Topography: you are 100 m from a tower, elevation angle $30°$. Height?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.9" dificuldade="modelagem">Solve $\cos x + \sin x = 1$ on $[0, 2\pi)$. (Use $\cos x + \sin x = \sqrt 2 \sin(x + \pi/4)$.)</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.10" dificuldade="desafio">Prove that in an equilateral triangle of side $\ell$, the area $= \ell^2 \sqrt 3 /4$ via the law of cosines.</Exercicio>

### Block B — Sequences (20.11 to 20.20)

<Exercicio numero="20.11" dificuldade="aplicacao">AP with $a_1 = 3$, $r = 5$. Compute $a_{20}$ and $S_{20}$.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.12" dificuldade="aplicacao">GP with $a_1 = 4$, $q = 3$. Compute $a_8$ and $S_8$.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.13" dificuldade="aplicacao">Compute $\sum_{n=0}^\infty (1/3)^n$.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.14" dificuldade="aplicacao">Determine whether the sequence $a_n = (n+1)/n$ converges. To what?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.15" dificuldade="aplicacao">Insert 4 terms forming an AP between 5 and 25.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.16" dificuldade="modelagem">You save R\$ 100 in the first month and grow 5% per month. Balance after 12 months?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.17" dificuldade="modelagem">A ball drops from 10 m and after each bounce rises 70% of the height. Total distance traveled?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.18" dificuldade="modelagem">Inflation: 0.5% per month compounded. How much after 12 months?</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.19" dificuldade="compreensao">Show that $0{,}999\ldots = 1$ via infinite GP.</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.20" dificuldade="desafio">Sequence $a_1 = 2$, $a_{n+1} = (a_n + 5)/2$. To which value does it converge?</Exercicio>

### Block C — Synthesis and challenges (20.21 to 20.25)

<Exercicio numero="20.21" dificuldade="desafio">In how much time does R\$ 1,000 double at 6% p.a. with continuous compounding? (Use $\ln$.)</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.22" dificuldade="desafio">Determine the general term and $S_n$ of the sequence $1, 4, 9, 16, 25, \ldots$ (it is neither AP nor GP).</Exercicio>
<Exercicio numero="20.23" dificuldade="desafio">Solve: $\sin x + \sin 2x + \sin 3x = 0$ on $[0, 2\pi)$.</Exercicio>
