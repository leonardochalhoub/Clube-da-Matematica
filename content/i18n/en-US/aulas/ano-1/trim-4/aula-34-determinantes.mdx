<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Computation and properties">

### 2x2

$$\det \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = ad - bc$$

### 3x3 (Sarrus)

$$\det \begin{pmatrix} a & b & c \\ d & e & f \\ g & h & i \end{pmatrix} = aei + bfg + cdh - ceg - bdi - afh$$

(Rule of "3 descending products − 3 ascending products".)

### Properties

1. $\det(I) = 1$.
2. $\det(A^T) = \det(A)$.
3. $\det(AB) = \det(A) \cdot \det(B)$.
4. $\det(\alpha A) = \alpha^n \det(A)$ for $A_{n \times n}$.
5. $\det(A^{-1}) = 1/\det(A)$.
6. **Swapping 2 rows/columns flips the sign**.
7. If $A$ has 2 equal rows/columns, $\det A = 0$.
8. Adding a multiple of one row to another **does not change** the determinant.

### Geometric interpretation

- $|\det A|$ = volume of the parallelepiped generated by the columns of $A$.
- $\det A > 0$: orientation preserved. $\det A < 0$: orientation reversed.
- $\det A = 0$: columns linearly dependent (parallelepiped "flattened").

### Invertibility criterion

$A$ invertible $\iff \det A \neq 0$.

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old">

A determinant is a **number** computed from a square matrix. For 2x2:

$$\det \begin{pmatrix} a & b \\ c & d \end{pmatrix} = ad - bc$$

For 3x3 use **Sarrus**: copy the first 2 columns to the right, add the 3 descending products, subtract the 3 ascending ones.

**Why does it matter?**

1. **Invertibility**: $\det A \neq 0$ ⟺ $A$ is invertible.
2. **Cramer**: the solution of $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$ uses ratios of determinants.
3. **Area and volume**: for 2x2, $|\det A|$ is the **area** of the parallelogram formed by the columns. For 3x3, it is the **volume**.

**Geometrically**: if the columns of $A$ are "vectors pointing in nearly the same direction", the parallelogram is thin and $\det A$ is small. If they are perpendicular, the parallelogram is "fat" and $\det A$ is large.

</Porta>
