You have completed **40 lessons** covering the foundations: functions, rate of change, trigonometry, sequences, intuitive limit, analytic geometry, vectors, linear systems, matrices, determinants, combinatorics, and probability.

### What you know now

- **Rigorous mathematical language**: sets, intervals, notation.
- **Modeling with functions**: linear, quadratic, exponential, logarithmic, trigonometric.
- **Pre-calculus**: average rate of change, intuitive limit of a sequence — ready for the formal calculus of Term 5-6.
- **Analytic geometry**: algebra-geometry connection via Descartes.
- **Introductory linear algebra**: vectors, matrices, systems, determinants.
- **Combinatorics + probability**: rigorous counting, Bayes.

### What's coming in Year 2

- Term 5: formal limits ($\eps$-$\delta$).
- Term 6: derivatives and rules (chain, product, quotient).
- Term 7: derivative applications (optimization, related rates).
- Term 8: descriptive statistics and deeper probability.

<Porta nivel="40" titulo="For a senior professional">

For a senior professional, this material is the **implicit substrate** of all technical work — almost never explicit, but always present.

### Map to advanced topics

- **Matrices** → spectral analysis, eigenvalues → PCA, dimensionality reduction, modal control, quantum mechanics.
- **Determinants** → volume form, Jacobian → integration on manifolds, Wronskian, ODE stability.
- **Combinatorics** → discrete structures → algorithms, complexity, integer optimization, cryptography.
- **Probability** → stochastic analysis → quantitative finance, optimal control, probabilistic ML, stochastic processes.

### Where this reappears

- **Quant**: Markowitz is matrix inverse of $\Sigma$. Binomial pricing is $(a+b)^n$. Risk-neutral is measure change.
- **ML**: backprop is the chain rule on matrices. Transformers are giant GEMMs. Bayesian inference is Bayes in high dim.
- **Robotics/CG**: rotations, projections, Jacobians.
- **Bio**: population models (Lotka-Volterra), genomics (counting, alignment), medical AI (Bayes).
- **Networks**: adjacency matrix, Laplacian spectrum, processes on graphs.

### Common workshop pitfalls

- Mixing $A_n^p$ and $\binom{n}{p}$.
- Forgetting to flip $\det A$ when swapping rows.
- Wrong sign in $(AB)^T = B^T A^T$.
- Confusing $P(A|B)$ with $P(B|A)$.
- Domain error in $\log$ or root.
- Signs in quadratic factoring.

### Essential task

Solve the 40 problems. This workshop is the Year 1 "roadmap test" — passing means a solid foundation. **Don't skip it.**
