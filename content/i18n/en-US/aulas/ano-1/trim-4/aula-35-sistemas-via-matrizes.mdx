<Porta nivel="formal" titulo="Solution methods">

### Matrix form

$\begin{cases} a_1 x + b_1 y + c_1 z = d_1 \\ a_2 x + b_2 y + c_2 z = d_2 \\ a_3 x + b_3 y + c_3 z = d_3 \end{cases}$ ⟺ $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$ with $A_{3 \times 3}$, $\mathbf{x}, \mathbf{b} \in \mathbb{R}^3$.

### Method 1 — Gaussian elimination

Elementary operations (do not alter the solution):
1. Swap two rows.
2. Multiply a row by a non-zero scalar.
3. Add a multiple of one row to another.

Goal: triangularize the augmented matrix $[A | \mathbf{b}]$ to row-echelon form. Then back-substitution.

### Method 2 — Cramer

For $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$ with $\det A \neq 0$:
$$x_i = \frac{\det A_i}{\det A}$$

where $A_i$ is $A$ with the $i$-th column replaced by $\mathbf{b}$.

### Method 3 — Inverse

$\mathbf{x} = A^{-1} \mathbf{b}$. One can compute $A^{-1}$ via $[A | I] \to [I | A^{-1}]$ by elimination.

### When to use each

- **Cramer**: theoretically elegant, but $O(n^4)$ — used only for $n \leq 3$.
- **Gauss**: $O(n^3)$, the practical standard.
- **Explicit inverse**: only if you need to solve several systems with the same $A$.

### Classification

- **Determined**: unique solution ($\det A \neq 0$).
- **Underdetermined**: infinitely many solutions ($\det A = 0$ + consistent).
- **Impossible**: no solution ($\det A = 0$ + inconsistent).

</Porta>

<Porta nivel="15" titulo="For a 15-year-old">

Linear system in matrix form: pack the coefficients in $A$, the unknowns in $\mathbf{x}$, and the results in $\mathbf{b}$. The system becomes $A\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{b}$.

To solve:

**Cramer**: ratio of determinants. Pretty, but only for small systems.

**Gauss** (elimination): perform operations that don't change the solution until the matrix is triangular, then solve from bottom to top.

Example:

$$\begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & | & 5 \\ 1 & 3 & | & 6 \end{pmatrix} \to \begin{pmatrix} 2 & 1 & | & 5 \\ 0 & 5/2 & | & 7/2 \end{pmatrix}$$

(I subtracted $L_1/2$ from $L_2$.) From the second row: $y = 7/5$. Substitute into the first: $2x = 5 - 7/5 \Rightarrow x = 9/5$.
